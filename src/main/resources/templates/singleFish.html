The Bluespotted Grouper

The bluespotted grouper is a species of marine ray-finned fish a member of the subfamily Epinephelinae: the groupers. These fish stand out with the blue spots that cover their body The species is extremely widely distributed, occurring in warm waters from the Red Sea to South Africa and east to French Polynesia and the Pitcairn group These are a predator species, they like warm waters and lay mostly motionless
